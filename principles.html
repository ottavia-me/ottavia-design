Product Principles

The rules the product team uses to say "yes" or "no" to every feature, screen and interaction. Not marketing. Not external positioning. The lens through which every decision gets filtered.

The One-Line Test
"Does this help the user understand something they couldn't see on their own, or does it just show them data they already have?"
If the answer is "just data," it doesn't belong in Ottavia.
Identity Principles
What kind of product is this
Principle 1

We are the interpretation layer, not the data layer

Ottavia does not compete with Garmin Connect, Oura, Apple Health or MyFitnessPal for data display. Those apps own data collection and presentation. Ottavia owns meaning.
  • We don't build dashboards that replicate what Garmin already shows
  • We don't show HRV as a number. We show what HRV means for this person today
  • When we show a metric, it's always in context: compared to something, connected to something, leading to something
  • A metric alone, without interpretation, is a bug in Ottavia
The test: If you cover the Ottavia logo and the screen could be mistaken for Garmin Connect, we've failed.
Principle 2

We build for understanding, not for compliance

Ottavia's goal is to make people smarter about their own body, not to make them follow instructions. The product succeeds when a user says "Now I get it" rather than "I did what the app told me."
  • Every recommendation includes the reasoning, not just the action
  • We show the "why" even when it takes more space
  • We don't gamify compliance (no streaks, no guilt for ignoring advice)
  • Over time, users should need Ottavia less for the basics, because they've internalized the patterns
The retention paradox: A product that makes itself less necessary? Yes. Because the depth keeps growing. The data accumulates. New patterns emerge. The user understands their basics but Ottavia keeps finding deeper connections they can't see alone.
Principle 3

We are a companion to your existing tools, not a replacement

Ottavia is the layer on top of the apps people already use. We make Garmin more valuable, not less. We make food logging more meaningful, not redundant.
  • We never ask users to abandon an app they already use
  • We integrate with, not duplicate, existing data sources
  • A user with three devices and five apps is our ideal user, not a problem to solve
Principle 4

We serve the person, not the metric

Metrics are inputs. The person's life is the output. Ottavia doesn't optimize HRV or sleep scores. It helps the person feel better, perform better and make decisions with confidence.
  • We never celebrate a metric improvement in isolation without connecting it to something the person cares about
  • Sometimes the "right" health decision conflicts with the person's life, and that's OK
  • We never shame, guilt or judge
Design Principles
How we build inside the product
Principle 5

Show meaning, not metrics

When Ottavia presents information, the default unit is insight, not number. Numbers appear in service of narrative, not as standalone displays.
Monitoring apps show Ottavia shows
HRV: 45ms Your recovery is lower than usual. After three hard training days and below-average sleep, your body is asking for a lighter day.
Sleep score: 72 You slept 6.5 hours but your deep sleep was only 45 minutes, below your baseline. This matters today because you have a long run planned.
Calories: 1,800 / 2,200 You're 400 calories short with a 15K run tomorrow. Under-fueling tonight usually shows up in your pace and RPE the next day.
Principle 6

Fewer screens, more depth

Every screen Ottavia adds is a screen competing with Garmin, Oura, MyFitnessPal. We don't want to win that fight. We want to avoid it.
  • The home screen is not a dashboard. It's a briefing: what matters right now, in words
  • Depth lives in the conversation, not in navigation hierarchy
  • If a user needs to tap through three screens to find value, we've designed it wrong
  • Every additional screen must justify itself: "Could the conversation handle this better?"
Principle 7

Proactive over reactive

The highest-value moments are when the system speaks first, not when the user asks. Ottavia reaches the user before they make a decision.
  • Push notifications are a first-class product surface, not an afterthought
  • The best Ottavia interaction is one the user didn't initiate but is grateful for
  • Each proactive message must pass the bar: "Would I thank Ottavia for interrupting me with this?"
  • Thoughtful friend, not noisy app
Principle 8

Time-aware, not just data-aware

Context is temporal. "Your sleep was bad" means different things at 7am (deciding whether to train), at noon (choosing lunch) and at 9pm (planning tomorrow).
  • The same data gets interpreted differently depending on when the user sees it
  • Morning: what's ahead. Evening: recovery and preparation
  • Ottavia knows what time it is and adjusts what it prioritizes accordingly
Principle 9

Progressive disclosure of complexity

New users need clarity. Experienced users want depth. The product must serve both.
  • First interactions are simple, warm and focused on the biggest insight
  • Over time, as data accumulates, responses get richer and more connected
  • Users who ask "why?" get explanations. Users who ask "what should I do?" get actions
  • The system adapts to how much complexity the user invites
Principle 10

Every output earns its existence

Nothing appears in Ottavia unless it passes a clear bar: does this help the user understand something or make a better decision?
  • No "good morning!" messages with no substance
  • No metric readouts without interpretation
  • No logging confirmations that just say "saved"
  • No notifications that could be described as "filler" or "engagement plays"
  • If we can't make it meaningful, we don't send it
Boundary Principles
What we intentionally refuse to build
Principle 11

We don't build dashboards

Dashboards are the monitoring era's product. They show you everything and help you with nothing. Garmin Connect is a dashboard. Oura is a dashboard. Apple Health is a dashboard. We are not.
  • No "metrics overview" screen with HRV + Sleep + Activity + Nutrition cards
  • No graphs for the sake of graphs
  • No "data explorer" where users browse their own metrics
  • No standalone "scores" or "readiness scores"
What this allows: Briefings that synthesize across data into plain language. Visualizations that appear inside conversation to support a specific point. Trend indicators when they're part of an interpretation.
Principle 12

We don't gamify health

No streaks. No badges. No points. No leaderboards. Gamification creates a compliance dynamic. Users chase the streak, not the understanding. When the streak breaks, they feel guilt. When they feel guilt, they avoid the app.
What we do instead: Show the value of consistency without gamifying it. "You've logged food consistently this week, which means your nutrition picture is clearer and more reliable." That's different from a streak counter.
Principle 13

We don't build social features

No sharing. No community. No "see how you compare to others." Health data is intimate. Comparison is toxic for most health contexts. Social features optimize for engagement, not understanding.
Principle 14

We don't duplicate source apps

If Garmin already shows your run map, we don't show a run map. If MyFitnessPal already shows your food diary, we don't show a food diary. If Oura already shows your readiness score, we don't show a readiness score. Our value is what happens after you've seen the data somewhere else and want to know what it means.
Principle 15

We don't chase feature parity

When a new feature appears in Whoop, Oura or any wearable app, the default response is: "That's their job, not ours." We only build features that serve our interpretation mission. Someone will always say "They just added X, shouldn't we have X too?" The answer is almost always no.
Principle 16

We accumulate, we don't snapshot

The longer you use Ottavia, the more valuable it becomes. The structured data model gets richer and the interpretations get more grounded. Monitoring apps are equally useful on day 1 and day 100 (which is why people churn). Ottavia should be noticeably better at month 6 than month 1. Every interaction should add something to the long-term model.
Principle 17

We speak in one voice

Users never see "agents." They never see seams between nutrition reasoning and sleep reasoning. Ottavia is one system, one personality, one voice. This shapes every design decision about how responses are structured.

The Prescriptive Boundary

Ottavia gives specific, opinionated answers when asked, but always shows the reasoning. That's opinionated AND educational. The user learns why, not just what.

"Skip the run today. Your HRV is at 32, you had 5.5 hours of sleep, and your training load this week is already above target. A rest day will serve your Saturday long run better."
