Question 1
Entry point — how logging starts
The composer already logs (type it, or tap the camera). The open question is whether a person mid-day, who just wants to log lunch, should have to think "chat" first. Three directions, from lightest to most dedicated.
Lean: keep the composer as the universal input (Direction A) and add one Home quick-action "Log a meal" (Direction B). The composer serves the conversational logger; the quick-action serves the "just record it" logger without asking them to compose a sentence. Avoid a floating FAB (Direction C) unless Home gets too crowded to surface the action inline — it duplicates the composer camera.
Direction A — Composer-native
Logging lives where the conversation lives. The camera and ＋ in the composer are the entry — no new surface. Lightest, already shipped.

Question 2
Is camera / voice / text a step?
The friction question. A modality chooser is explicit and teachable, but it's an extra screen between "I want to log" and "it's logged." The alternative is that modality is never a decision — one field quietly accepts a photo, a held mic, or typed words.
Lean: modality is not a step (Direction B) as the default — the composer already accepts all three, so a chooser would be a regression. Keep a camera-first sheet (Direction C) as the Home quick-action's target, because "photo" is the single fastest real-world path and deserves to open instantly, with voice/text one tap away — no blocking chooser screen (Direction A).
Direction A — Chooser is a step
Tapping "Log" opens a sheet: Photo · Say it · Type it. Explicit and self-teaching, but it's one more tap before you've logged anything.

Question 3
After an item is selected — what we show, and the expert
Two philosophies: confirm-then-log (review a card, adjust portion, then commit) vs log-then-context (commit fast, show how it fits — the current pattern). Then the new layer: a named expert's opinion, so the meal isn't just counted, it's coached. Communication Type 2 says a confirmation must show how the item fits goals — never just "saved."
Lean: log-then-context stays the spine (Direction B) — it's fast and matches the current saved-meal detail. Add the expert opinion as an optional, earned layer (Direction C): shown when a relevant expert has a genuine, specific take (post-run recovery window, protein already met), never as filler on every log. Confirm-card (Direction A) is the right pre-log step only for photo logs, where portion/identification is uncertain.
Direction A — Confirm & adjust (pre-log)
For photo logs: show what was detected, let the portion be nudged, then commit. Handles the "is this right?" moment before it's counted.

Product thinking
Hard problems & what to test first
1 · Fast vs. correct

Log-then-context is fast but risks logging a wrong portion. Confirm-first is accurate but adds a tap every meal.

→ Split by modality: photo = confirm first (uncertain), text/voice = log then context.
2 · When does the expert speak?

An expert opinion on every single log becomes noise and erodes trust ("does she really have a take on my snack?").

→ Gate it: only when a relevant expert + a specific, data-grounded insight exists. Otherwise show context alone.
3 · Which expert, and is it earned?

The take must be attributable and non-generic — a real dietitian's angle, not "eat balanced." Wrong expert on the wrong meal breaks the premium feel.

→ Match expert to domain (nutrition→Nancy Clark); "Why this?" reveals the data behind it.
4 · No medical claims

Expert opinions can drift toward prescriptive/clinical language. Ottavia informs, never diagnoses.

→ Opinion = observation + fit to goals. Never "you should," never a health claim.
