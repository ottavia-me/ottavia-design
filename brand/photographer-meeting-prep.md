# Ottavia × Elad — Creative Shoot Brief
### Meeting Prep for Photographer & Cinematographer
**Date:** April 20, 2026 | **Prepared by:** Vika Daniel (CPO)

## What You Need to Know About Ottavia First

Before we talk about a single shot, the photographer and cinematographer need to understand what Ottavia *actually is* — because it changes everything about how we shoot.

**Ottavia is not a fitness app.**

It's a personal reasoning system for wellbeing. It ingests data from wearables (Garmin, Apple Health), nutrition logs, biomarkers, sleep, hydration, and more — and surfaces connections the user couldn't see on their own. Think: "Your HRV dropped to 30, you slept 5 hours, and you ran yesterday. Don't run today." Not a dashboard. Not a chatbot. A knowledgeable peer who remembers everything and tells you the truth.

**The brand attributes:**
Grounded. Specific. Direct. Quietly confident. Warm through precision. Intelligent. Personal. Honest.

**What Ottavia is NOT:**
Corporate wellness. Fitness bro aesthetic. Gamified. Hype-driven. Medical authority.

**The visual feeling we're building toward:**
Opening a well-made notebook. Tactile. Considered. Quietly premium. Not a tech dashboard, not a clinical interface — something that respects the seriousness of the data without feeling sterile.

**Color palette reference:** Warm creams (#f5f1ea), soft tan (#ccc8b2), deep ink darks. Muted and analog, not saturated and sporty.

---

## Who Is Elad

**Elad is User 1** — the founder of Ottavia, and the first person the product was built for.

He's an ultrarunner and entrepreneur. He built Ottavia because he lived the problem: tracking enormous amounts of training data, nutrition, recovery — and having no system smart enough to connect it all into something actionable. Ottavia is the product he needed and couldn't find.

This is not a spokesperson shoot. This is a documentary of the person the product was built around. The authenticity is everything. His gear, his actual trails, his real kitchen, his actual Garmin on his actual wrist.

**The narrative thread:** Elad pushes hard. He recovers smart. Ottavia is the intelligence layer between those two things.

---

## The Core Creative Concept

> **"The data behind the human."**

Every shot has two layers: Elad as a physical, embodied person — running, resting, eating, breathing — and Ottavia as the intelligence quietly tracking what it all means. We don't need to show the app in every frame. But every scene should carry the subtext: *there is meaning being made here.*

This is editorial-documentary meets personal brand. Think: a high-quality runner profile in Outside Magazine, not a fitness product ad. The product enters the frame when it earns it — not as a prop, but as a natural moment.

## Shot Categories & Specific Ideas

### 1. RUNNING

The most important visual territory. Elad is an ultrarunner — this is where his identity lives and where Ottavia is most viscerally relevant.

**Concept shots:**

- **"The morning decision"** — Elad at the trailhead, Garmin strapped, checking his wrist before starting. Ottavia just told him something — his HRV, his recovery score. He knows something before the first step. Close-up on the watch face, then wide on his face making the call.

- **"Mid-run data"** — Caught in motion, the Garmin visible but incidental. Not posed. Real pace, real exertion. We want to see the body working — jaw tension, arm drive, sweat. The device is on his wrist the way it always is.

- **"The finish"** — Not triumphant arms in the air. The real finish: bent over, hands on knees, HR coming down. The raw moment before the analysis starts. This is the shot that earns the product — because what happens *next* (Ottavia interpreting the effort) is the entire value proposition.

- **"Rest day obedience"** — Elad at the trailhead. Gear on. *Not running.* Phone out, looking at the Ottavia screen. He wanted to run. It said don't. He didn't. That tension — in his body language, in the stillness — is the story.

- **Slow motion b-roll:** Feet on trail, sweat mid-air, Garmin strap in motion, stride mechanics from below and behind. Shot at 120fps+ for post flexibility.

**Locations:** Real trails he actually runs. Not a treadmill, not a track, not a public park with visible branded equipment. Natural terrain — dirt, roots, elevation. Dawn or golden hour light.

---

### 2. RECOVERY

This is where Ottavia's intelligence is most visible. Recovery is invisible to the naked eye — which is exactly what makes it a powerful visual territory.

**Concept shots:**

- **"HRV morning"** — Elad waking up, still in bed, immediately checking Ottavia. Not scrolling Instagram — the first thing he reaches for is data. The screen is warm in dim light. His face is calm, reading. This is the most intimate shot in the whole set.

- **"The debrief"** — Post-run, sitting on a step or log, phone in hand, reading his Ottavia report. Elbows on knees, focused. Not happy, not sad — *processing*. The app has something to say and he's listening.

- **"Recovery at rest"** — Legs elevated, on the couch or floor, Garmin still on, phone nearby. The stillness after effort. This shot communicates that rest is *active* when you're doing it right — and Ottavia is the reason he's resting instead of running again.

- **Stretching with data** — Foam roller or mobility work, phone propped where he can see it. The body and the data, side by side.

---

### 3. EATING & NUTRITION

Ottavia tracks nutrition deeply (14 dimensions including macros, timing, food composition). This is daily life, not "clean eating" aesthetic.

**Concept shots:**

- **"The log"** — Elad mid-meal, logging food in Ottavia. Not a perfect meal. Real food. The shot is about the ritual of logging — the moment of connecting what he's eating to what it means.

- **"Breakfast and the overnight data"** — Coffee in hand, reading Ottavia's overnight report at the kitchen table. Morning light. The connection between last night's data and today's first meal.

- **"Post-run refuel"** — Real food, real quantities. Protein, carbs — the specific numbers matter to him because Ottavia made them matter. Maybe the app is visible in the background on the counter, showing his macro targets.

- **Close-up details:** A fork mid-bite. A glass of water next to the phone showing hydration tracking. Hands on food. Nothing styled, nothing hero-shot.

**Important:** This should feel like an athlete's kitchen, not a food photographer's studio. Functional, lived-in, real.

---

### 4. DRINKING / HYDRATION

Ottavia tracks hydration (one of the 14 dimensions). Small behavior, big data story.

**Concept shots:**

- **"300ml logged"** — Elad drinking from a water bottle, Ottavia notification visible: he's at 300ml and his run is in 90 minutes. The mundane act of drinking water becomes a data event.

- **"The electrolyte decision"** — Post-run, making a hydration decision based on what Ottavia surfaced. He's not guessing — he knows.

---

### 5. THE ENTREPRENEUR LAYER

Elad isn't just a runner. He's a founder. Ottavia serves both identities — the athlete *and* the person running a company, managing stress, sleep debt, cognitive load.

**Concept shots:**

- **"Before the call"** — Laptop open, Ottavia in another window or on his phone. He just checked his HRV before a high-stakes meeting. The logic is the same as before a run: know your state before you perform.

- **"The intersection"** — Running gear next to a laptop. Two worlds that Ottavia connects. Can be environmental — no people needed.

- **"End of the day check-in"** — Winding down. Screen glow, Ottavia's daily summary. What happened today, across training *and* work. This is the product's promise in a single image.

---

### 6. BRAND / ENVIRONMENTAL DETAILS

Shots with no Elad — for design use, website headers, abstract storytelling.

- **Device close-ups:** Garmin watch face, Ottavia screen visible (data, not UI chrome), phone on table
- **Trail textures:** Dirt, roots, stone — matched to brand's warm muted palette
- **Morning light abstracts:** Sunrise through trees, warm tones, long shadows
- **Stillness:** Empty trail, quiet kitchen, the before-and-after of effort

## Overall Visual Strategy

### Photography Style Direction

| Direction | What it means in practice |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Natural light first** | Golden hour, blue hour, diffused morning light. No ring lights, no harsh flash |
| **Real locations** | His actual trails, his actual home. No rented studios, no generic gym environments |
| **Editorial documentary** | Catch real moments. If something needs to be staged, it should *not look staged* |
| **Muted, warm color grade** | Match the brand palette. Creams, tans, deep greens on trail. Avoid saturated, sporty filters |
| **Depth over sharpness** | Shallow depth of field — the story is in the subject, not the environment |
| **Device as supporting actor** | The Garmin and phone appear naturally, the way they actually do in his life. Never prop-forward |

**The test for every shot:** Does this feel like it was photographed *because it was true*, or photographed *to make a point*? It should always feel like the former.

### Photography: What to Avoid

- Stock-photo fitness energy (perfect lighting, perfect form, white background)
- Forced product placement (holding phone up toward camera, unnatural angle)
- Generic "entrepreneur at laptop" shots
- Bright, clean, clinical aesthetics
- Anything that could be anyone — every shot should feel specifically *Elad*

## Video / Film Direction

### The Two Asset Types

**1. Brand Film (60–90 seconds, horizontal)**

One short film that tells Elad's story. Narration should be Elad's actual voice — not scripted, but pulled from a recorded conversation. The film connects his training life to the intelligence Ottavia provides. It's not a product demo. It's a portrait.

Structure idea:
- Open on effort (the physical reality of ultrarunning)
- Introduce the question (how do you know when to push and when to stop?)
- Introduce Ottavia not as software but as the answer to that question
- Close on a moment of trust — he looked at the data and made a decision

**2. Social Cuts (15–30 seconds, vertical 9:16)**

Modular scenes that can stand alone as Instagram Reels, Stories, or short-form content. Each one should communicate one idea:
- "Ottavia told him not to run. He listened."
- "Every meal logged. Not because of discipline. Because now it means something."
- "He checked his HRV before the investor call the same way he checks it before a race."

### Film Technical Asks

- **Camera:** Cinema camera preferred (Sony FX3, BMPCC 6K, or equivalent). Full-frame sensor for natural light performance.
- **Slow motion:** 120fps+ for running sequences. We want the ability to slow down effort — the strike of a foot, the expression mid-climb.
- **Sound capture:** Separate audio. We want ambient sound — footsteps on trail, breathing, kitchen sounds — available in post. This is not a music video.
- **Interview / conversation capture:** A recorded conversation with Elad (not a scripted interview) that can provide voice-over material. Loose format — ask him about ultrarunning, about decisions, about what it means to know your body.

## Cast: Just Elad, or Add a Woman?

Short answer: **just Elad for this shoot.** Plan a second shoot with a woman for phase 2.

Here's the reasoning.

The entire power of this shoot is the founder-as-user-1 story. Elad built the product because he lived the problem. That's a specific, non-replicable narrative, and it's most credible when it's undiluted. Splitting the shoot's story between two people weakens both.

Adding a woman to this shoot, unless she's someone who genuinely uses Ottavia and whose presence is natural, risks looking like diversity casting — which is exactly the kind of inauthenticity the brand can't afford. The audience (serious Garmin users) will feel the difference between a real person and a prop.

That said, the brand absolutely needs a woman in the library, and soon. Ottavia's brand soul is internally described as "a mother" — there's a feminine intelligence at the core of the product. Showing only a male ultrarunner as the face, long term, signals "built for men" even when the product isn't. Fabio and Lorenzo's content can help in the short term (Fabio is male, but Lorenzo's network skews sports-mixed), but a dedicated shoot with a female Ottavia user should be planned for phase 2 — ideally a runner or endurance athlete who's been using the app for a few weeks and has genuine reactions to share.

**For this shoot: Elad only, full focus, full authenticity.**
**Phase 2 shoot: a real female user, her own story, not a mirror of Elad's.**
